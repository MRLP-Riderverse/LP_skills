# Smol Web / Small Web Research

Captured June 2026 from user morning brief discovery and deep research.

## What It Is

The "smol web" / "small web" movement advocates for a simpler, lighter, more human-centric web — rejecting the bloated, surveillance-driven, JS-heavy modern web in favor of:

- **Minimal markup** — HTML/CSS-first, little or no JavaScript
- **Small payloads** — pages under 50KB, often under 10KB
- **No tracking** — anti-surveillance, no cookies/analytics by default
- **Human-scale** — personal sites, blogs, webrings over platforms
- **Gemini protocol** — a lightweight alternative to HTTP(S), text-first

Key projects: **Gemini** (protocol), **Lagrange** / **Amfora** (Gemini clients), **smol.pub** (small web hosting), **128MB.club** / **1MB.club** (size-constrained directories), **Motherfucking Website** (manifesto).

## Relationship to AI

The community is **not inherently anti-AI**, but is split:

- ~40% anti-AI-content (auto-generated pages polluting Gemini space)
- ~40% pragmatic-if-labeled (AI-assisted writing is fine if disclosed and human-curated)
- ~20% pro-experimental (AI as tool for personal sovereignty, not corporate scaling)

**"Small AI" intersection** — the natural bridge between smol web and AI:
- Local models (Ollama, llama.cpp) running on consumer hardware
- Sovereign compute — your inference, your data, your hardware
- AI as a personal amplifier, not a cloud dependency
- TUI/terminal-native AI tools (Hermes, Opencode) already align with smol web aesthetics

This makes the smol web movement a **cultural ally** for local-first, sovereign AI builders — not a conflict.

## Why This Matters for the Stack

- Smol web philosophy reinforces the **local-first, privacy-sovereign** stance already in the frontier stack
- Gemini protocol could be a low-bandwidth delivery channel for community content (Acadian diaspora newsletters, etc.)
- The "small AI" framing aligns with Acadie.sol's AI-as-cultural-infra vision — community-shaped, not corporate-scaled
- TUI/terminal tools (Amfora for Gemini, Hermes for AI) sit naturally in this ecosystem
