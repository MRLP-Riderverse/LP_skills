# New Brunswick Pay Transparency Act — session notes

## Primary sources used
- New Brunswick statutes: https://laws.gnb.ca/en/document/as/2026,%20c.1
- GNB news/search result surfaced via DuckDuckGo: "Pay Transparency Act introduced" (March 18, 2026)
- GNB employment standards page: "Rules of payment and payroll records including equal pay for equal work"

## Key takeaways
- This is **pay transparency / compensation disclosure** legislation, not a payroll processing method.
- Act purpose: reduce systemic wage disparities, increase compensation transparency, protect employees from reprisals, and support equity/DEI goals.
- Core obligations include:
  - job postings include expected hourly wage or salary, or a range
  - employers do not seek compensation history except where publicly accessible
  - reporting employers prepare annual pay transparency reports
- Reporting employer threshold: **50+ employees** (average per calendar year).
- Filing deadlines in the statute:
  - 100+ employees: first report due Mar 31, 2029
  - 50–99 employees: first report due Mar 31, 2030
- Enforcement exists via inspectors, orders, administrative penalties, appeals, and offences.
- Important nuance: the Act was assented June 12, 2026, but several operative sections commence **on proclamation**.

## Practical interpretation pattern
- Frame the issue as **legibility + compliance** rather than payroll mechanics.
- Check whether the law changes disclosure/reporting obligations, not just payment timing.
- When the user asks how a law fits their philosophy, translate it into system properties:
  - transparency
  - auditability
  - power asymmetry reduction
  - proof over vibe
