## Cross-Relation Pattern (Acadie ↔ Acadiana ↔ Nouvelle-Aquitaine)

User frames these as "same patch" peoples — shared ancestry from the Grand Dérangement era and earlier (Gascon/Béarnais migration to Acadie pre-1755).

| Region | Media Health | Evidence |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Acadie (NB/NS) | Healthy | Radio-Canada, Acadie Nouvelle, substantial FR output |
| Acadiana (LA) | Thin | Near-zero French RSS; English-only ecosystem dominant |
| Nouvelle-Aquitaine | Near-zero | Gascon/Occitan invisible; French standard only |

### Shared Pattern Signals
1. **Rural désenclavement** — all three fight the same geography-of-isolation battle
2. **Cultural survival through festivals** — Toros y Salsa ↔ Cajun-Zydeco ↔ Fête nationale de l'Acadie
3. **Language erosion gradient** — the further from the Dérangement, the quieter the mother tongue
4. **Self-sovereignty instinct** — Béarn digital pivot, Acadiana workforce branding, Acadie pro-localization
5. **Cultural queen/pageant as diplomacy** — Lafourche Queens ↔ Acadian cultural ambassadors
6. **Infrastructure fight** — LGV Sud-Ouest ↔ NB rail debates ↔ LA flooding resilience
7. **Sovereign infrastructure convergence** — both "mother homes" (Acadiana + NA) are building sovereign tech infrastructure simultaneously (Dax data center, Foxconn €450M semi fab). User sees this as a bridge opportunity — "both my mother homes are moving in on sovereign future"
