# Music & Artist Monitor Skill

A reusable skill for tracking music industry developments, artist updates, releases, and trends - particularly focused on web3 music adoption, distribution innovation, and specific artist interests.

## Key Learning from Execution
Through practical application, the most effective approach involves using structured news sources (like Google News RSS) with targeted queries, then applying a rigorous evaluation framework to separate meaningful developments from hype. The skill works best when focused on practical utility and real-world adoption rather than speculative potential.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you need to:
- Stay updated on specific artist news (Green Day, BMTH, Jaden, Joji, etc.)
- Monitor music distribution platforms and innovations
- Track web3 music projects like Audius, Royal, Sound.xyz, etc.
- Follow ecommerce platforms for creators (Fourthwall, Shopify, etc.)
- Get insights on music industry trends and adoption
- Receive personalized music news briefings

## Components

This skill includes:
1. System prompt defining the music monitoring approach
2. Research task specification for artist/industry monitoring
3. Builder philosophy principles (adapted for music/creative focus)
4. Output template for consistent reporting
5. Question pack for targeted music analysis

## How to Use

### As a Research Task
Load this skill and provide the research question:
```
Load the music-artist-monitor skill and research: [your specific music/artist question]
```

### For Weekly Briefings
Set up a cron job to run this skill weekly with artist/music focus.

### Practical Execution Approach
When running this skill, the following approach has proven effective:
1. Use Google News RSS feeds for reliable, structured news gathering
2. Search specific queries for each artist/topic area (e.g., "Green Day new album tour 2024 2025")
3. Filter results to extract meaningful headlines and filter out boilerplate
4. Apply the evaluation framework to assess practical utility vs hype
5. Organize findings using the skill's output format sections
6. Focus on what's actually working/adopted vs experimental

## Evaluation Framework

When analyzing music industry developments, the skill prompts assessment of:
- **Artist activity**: New releases, tours, collaborations, announcements
- **Distribution innovation**: New platforms, web3 adoption, direct-to-fan models
- **Fan engagement**: Novel interaction models, community building, monetization
- **Technology integration**: How music tech intersects with broader trends
- **Practical usability**: What actually works for artists and fans today
- **Sustainability**: Long-term viability of new models and platforms
- **Cultural impact**: Influence on music creation, consumption, and community

## Standard Monitoring Areas

The skill is configured to monitor:
- **Specific Artists**: Green Day, Bring Me The Horizon (BMTH), Jaden, Joji, and user-specified interests
- **Music Distribution**: Traditional (Spotify, Apple Music), emerging (Audius, Royal, Sound.xyz), and creator platforms
- **Web3 Music Adoption**: NFT music, tokenized royalties, fan tokens, decentralized streaming
- **Creator Ecommerce**: Fourthwall, Shopify for artists, Patreon alternatives, merch innovation
- **Industry Trends**: Sync licensing, live streaming evolution, social music experiences
- **Music Tech**: Production tools, AI in music, analytics platforms

## Output Format

Results follow a structured format with:
- Artist-specific updates and news
- Distribution/platform developments
- Web3 music adoption insights
- Creator economy trends
- Practical recommendations for artists/fans
- Plain-language summary of what's actually happening
